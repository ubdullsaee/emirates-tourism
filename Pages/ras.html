About Ras Al Khaimah

Ras Al Khaimah (RAK), one of the seven emirates that make up the United Arab Emirates (UAE), is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and growing economic development. Situated in the northernmost part of the UAE, it is bordered by the Arabian Gulf to the west and the Hajar Mountains to the east.

Facts About Ajman

Population

Home to over 300,000 residents. A mix of Emiratis and expatriates, contributing to a diverse cultural environment.

History

It has been inhabited for thousands of years, with evidence of settlements dating back to the Bronze Age. Historically, the emirate was known for its pearling industry, which was a significant source of wealth before the discovery of oil.

Governance

Governed by the Al Qasimi family, with His Highness Sheikh Saud bin Saqr Al Qasimi as the current ruler. Vision 2030: The emirate has a strategic vision to enhance its economic growth, infrastructure, and social development.

Culture

Emphasizes preserving its traditional heritage and culture, with regular cultural festivals and events. Balances modernization with the preservation of its historical and cultural identity.

Economy

Diversification Unlike some other emirates, RAK's economy is less dependent on oil and more diversified, with key sectors including tourism, manufacturing, and real estate.
Free Trade Zones Home to several free trade zones, which attract international businesses with tax incentives and other benefits.
