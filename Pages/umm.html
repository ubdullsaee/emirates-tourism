About Umm AL-Quwain

Umm Al-Quwain is one of the seven emirates that make up the United Arab Emirates (UAE). It is located in the northern part of the country, nestled between the emirates of Sharjah and Ras Al Khaimah. Despite being one of the smallest and least populous emirates, Umm Al-Quwain boasts a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and a relaxed lifestyle.

Facts About Umm AL-Quawin

Local Festivals

Al-Quwain celebrates traditional Emirati festivals, including National Day and cultural heritage festivals, showcasing local customs, music, and dance.

Quiet and Relaxed

Known for its laid-back atmosphere compared to the hustle and bustle of larger emirates like Dubai and Abu Dhabi.

Governance

The emirate is ruled by the Al Mualla family, with the current ruler being Sheikh Saud bin Rashid Al Mualla. Focused on sustainable development, improving infrastructure, and enhancing the quality of life for residents and visitors.

Community-Oriented

Strong sense of community with traditional Emirati values and hospitality.

Economy

The emirate is developing its tourism sector, with a focus on eco-tourism, water sports, and heritage tourism. The Ahmed Bin Rashid Port and the Free Trade Zone also contribute to the local economy by attracting businesses and investors.
