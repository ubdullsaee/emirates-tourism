About Ajman

Ajman is one of the seven emirates that make up the United Arab Emirates (UAE). It is the smallest emirate in terms of area but offers a unique blend of traditional and modern attractions.

Facts About Ajman

Traditional Souks

Ajman boasts traditional markets, or souks, where visitors can purchase local goods, spices, and crafts.

Urban Development

Ajman is witnessing rapid urban development with new residential, commercial, and infrastructure projects, enhancing its appeal as a place to live and work.

Industrial

Ajman is known for its manufacturing and industrial sectors, with Ajman Industrial Area and Ajman Free Zone being key economic zones.

Port and Free Zone

Port of Ajman and Ajman Free Zone play crucial roles in trade and logistics, attracting numerous businesses and contributing to the emirate's economy.

Tourism

Beaches Ajman is famous for its pristine beaches along the Arabian Gulf, offering a serene environment for relaxation and water activities.
Museums Ajman Museum, housed in an 18th-century fort, provides insights into the emirate's history and heritage.
Cultural Sites The Al-Murabba Watchtower and other historical landmarks showcase the emirate's rich cultural heritage.
