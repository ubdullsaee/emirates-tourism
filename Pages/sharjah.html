About Sharjah

Sharjah is located on the Arabian Gulf coast and shares borders with Dubai to the south, Ajman to the north, and Ras Al Khaimah to the east. It also extends inland to the Gulf of Oman.

Facts About Sharjah

Cultural Capital

Sharjah has been designated as the Cultural Capital of the Arab World by UNESCO in 1998 and the Capital of Islamic Culture for 2014 by the Organisation of Islamic Cooperation. It is known for its commitment to preserving and promoting Arab and Islamic culture.

Economic Sectors

Sharjah's economy is diverse, with key sectors including manufacturing, real estate, tourism, and logistics. The emirate is known for its free zones, such as the Sharjah Airport International Free Zone (SAIF Zone) and Hamriyah Free Zone, which attract international businesses.

Infrastructure

Sharjah has implemented various initiatives to promote environmental sustainability and conservation. The emirate focuses on waste management, recycling programs, and the preservation of natural habitats.

Governance

Sharjah is ruled by His Highness Sheikh Dr. Sultan bin Muhammad Al Qasimi, who has been in power since 1972. Under his leadership, the emirate has seen significant development in infrastructure, culture, and education.
